Routine

My Weekly Split

Here's how I structure my training week. Each day focuses on a specific muscle group, with nested warm-up protocols inside each session:

  1. Monday — Push (Chest, Shoulders, Triceps)
    • Bench Press: 4 sets × 6–8 reps
    • Overhead Press: 3 sets × 8 reps
    • Lateral Raises: 3 sets × 12 reps
    • Tricep Pushdowns: 3 sets × 12 reps
  2. Tuesday — Pull (Back, Biceps)
    • Deadlift: 4 sets × 5 reps
    • Pull-ups: 4 sets to failure
    • Barbell Row: 3 sets × 8 reps
    • Hammer Curls: 3 sets × 10 reps
  3. Wednesday — Rest or Active Recovery
    • 20–30 min walk or light cardio
    • Foam rolling and mobility work
  4. Thursday — Legs
    • Squat: 4 sets × 6–8 reps
    • Romanian Deadlift: 3 sets × 10 reps
    • Leg Press: 3 sets × 12 reps
    • Calf Raises: 4 sets × 15 reps
  5. Friday — Full Body / Accessory
    • Dips: 3 sets × 10 reps
    • Face Pulls: 3 sets × 15 reps
    • Plank: 3 × 60 seconds
  6. Saturday & Sunday — Rest
    • Prioritize sleep and nutrition
    • Optional: recreational sports or hiking

Nutrition

Fueling the Grind

Training hard means nothing without fueling right. Here are the nutrition principles I follow:

  • High protein intake — aim for ~0.8–1g per lb of bodyweight
  • Calorie surplus on training days, maintenance on rest days
  • Prioritize whole foods: lean meats, eggs, rice, oats, and vegetables
  • Hydration — minimum 3 liters of water per day
  • Creatine monohydrate — 5g daily (well-researched and effective)
  • Sleep — 7–9 hours for maximum muscle repair and recovery
